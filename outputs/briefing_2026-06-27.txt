## BULL VS BEAR

**BULLISH CASE**
1. **Extreme Oversold Technicals** — Silver's 50% crash and 6-month lows have pushed the market into textbook oversold territory, historically signaling capitulation and reversal potential.
2. **Strong Long-Term Fundamentals** — Sprott's positive long-term outlook and analyst commentary on "very limited downside" suggest institutional conviction in silver's eventual rebound as macro conditions stabilize.
3. **Industrial Demand Untouched** — No mention of demand destruction; the selloff is purely macro/positioning driven, leaving supply-demand fundamentals intact for eventual recovery.

**BEARISH CASE**
1. **Structural Macro Headwinds** — Rising yields and dollar strength are self-reinforcing headwinds that will persist until Fed policy or inflation dynamics shift materially.
2. **Debasement Trade Unwinding** — Reversal of inflation hedging flows suggests investors are rotating away from precious metals, not temporarily rebalancing; this is a regime shift.
3. **ING Downgrade Signals Institutional Shift** — Major analyst house cutting forecasts indicates professional money is turning defensive; retail and momentum may follow.

**VERDICT:** The bearish case has the stronger near-term conviction today. While technical oversold conditions offer tactical bounce potential, the structural macro drivers (rising yields, dollar strength, and institutional de-risking) remain pointed downward until we see evidence of policy or inflation expectations stabilizing.
