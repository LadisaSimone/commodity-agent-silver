## MARKET ANALYSIS

### Current Price & Recent Movement
Silver is trading at **$59.22, up +2.59 (+4.57%)** as of today, following a volatile period. The metal crashed to 6-month lows in mid-week (~$57/oz by June 26), marking the first time below $60 since December 2025. This represents approximately a **50% decline from recent highs**, creating an "oversold" technical condition. Gold/Silver ratio has expanded to 68.9, reflecting silver's underperformance relative to gold.

### Key Market Drivers
**Headwinds:**
- **Rising interest rates and stronger US dollar** — ING's downgrade cites these as primary structural headwinds, reducing precious metals' appeal
- **Rate hike expectations** — Cited as a trigger for the broader selloff earlier in the week
- **ETF outflows** — Forced liquidation pressure from fund selling, contributing to the downward momentum
- **Unwinding of "debasement" trade** — The rally in precious metals and bitcoin has reversed as inflation concerns ease

**Tailwinds:**
- **Extreme oversold technical conditions** — Multiple sources note the 50% crash and suggest "very limited downside" from current levels
- **Long-term institutional support** — Sprott maintains strong conviction on long-term silver outlook despite volatility
- **Bounce recovery** — Today's +4.57% rally suggests relief from capitulation lows

### Notable News
- No disruptions to mining supply detected in reporting period
- Market sentiment reflects debate between short-term technical damage and long-term fundamental demand
- Analyst commentary increasingly focuses on entry points for value investors

Silver appears to be consolidating after a sharp washout. The recovery today signals potential stabilization near $57-60 range, with key technical support being tested. Macro conditions (rates, dollar strength) remain
